# Clockfield Quantum Information Theory

This repository contains two papers exploring how information, probability, and physical reality emerge from the mathematics of the Clockfield—a nonlinear scalar field where local proper time freezes under geometric frustration.

Rather than treating information theory and quantum mechanics as separate mysteries, these papers propose that both are natural consequences of the universe acting as a self-organizing, error-correcting system.

## The Papers

### 1. [Frustration, Crystallisation, and the Substrate-Independent Grammar of Adaptive Information](clockfield_information_theory.md)
This paper connects Claude Shannon's Information Theory and John Archibald Wheeler's "It-from-Bit" universe to the Clockfield metric. It shows that the mechanism of *frustration causing a permanent, frozen structural change* is a universal, substrate-independent rule. It argues that physical particles, biological evolution (DNA), human cognition, and AI learning (neural networks) all use the exact same mathematical geometry to adapt, survive, and store information out of chaotic noise.

### 2. [The Probability of the Bit: How the Clockfield Freeze Threshold Generates Quantum Mechanics](clockfield_bit_qm.md)
An addendum to the first paper. It demonstrates that we do not need to invent separate postulates for Quantum Mechanics. The famous "Born Rule" (which calculates quantum probability) emerges automatically from the Clockfield framework. When a wave hits the exact threshold where time freezes, the statistical chance of it crossing that threshold perfectly matches the probabilities we observe in quantum physics. In this view, there is no magical quantum "collapse"—there is only a geometric threshold.
